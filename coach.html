Her story

She didn't have it
figured out either.

As a teenager and young adult, Janet wrestled with identity the way a lot of people do — quietly, privately, without a language for what she was carrying. She looked around rooms and wondered who to talk to. She felt the itch of being occupied without direction. Days she felt overwhelmed. Days of just gliding through life, taken by the tides, marking time rather than choosing a direction.

It felt like everyone was supposed to figure it out by themselves. It looked like everyone else had figured it out too. Nobody sat down and said: here is who you are, here is what you stand for, here is where you could go. So she learned it the long way — through faith, through honest reflection, through the kind of work that doesn't show up on a CV.

"I was busy. But was I actually going anywhere? There were days I was just gliding — taken by the tides. I didn't want other young people to take as long to find their footing as I did."

Once she found her footing, she couldn't stop wanting that for other people. Especially young people. That's where the burden came from — not from a training module, but from the personal memory of what it felt like to need someone to reach out, and to have no one come.

That burden has taken her across Kenya and into other countries as a missionary and trainer — into high schools, university campuses, churches, and community spaces. She visits campuses regularly as a speaker. She has been in youth leadership and mentorship for over two decades. And she has carried one desire through all of it: that young people would have the kind of character that speaks before they say a word. Integrity. Clarity. A life lived from the inside out.

Thrive is her invitation to do in three days what took her years — with the right space, the right questions, and someone who has genuinely walked the same path.

What drives her

She is still
in the rooms.

Janet isn't someone who ran a programme once and now talks about it. She is currently in mentoring young people, on the board of a pan-African youth movement, and regularly in schools and campuses as a speaker. She is still doing the work — right now, while building Thrive.

She has stood on mission fields watching lives change at baptism, her heart almost too full to hold. She has sat with young people in the most vulnerable communities in Nairobi, training caregivers and parents to raise children with purpose and dignity. She has designed learning programmes for organisations and lectured at universities.

But what she brings to Thrive is none of the credentials. What she brings is the lived memory of the itch — and the deep, personal conviction that every young person in the room deserves someone to finally walk them through it.

"This is life's purpose — pointing men and women to Christ Jesus and preparing them to meet Him when He comes." — Janet, on the banks of River Weija · jtoyende.wordpress.com
