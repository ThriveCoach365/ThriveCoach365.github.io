Weekly Formulae · Teen Life

A formula for
every day of the week.

Seven days. Seven frameworks. Each one anchored in Scripture and designed to give young people a practical, memorable way to approach each day — from Monday's mindset to Sunday's worship. Pick one. Practice it. Then come back for the next.

Success Sunday
The W.A.I.T. Strategy
for Patience
Waiting on God renews your strength
"Those who hope in the Lord will renew their strength." — Isaiah 40:31

Sunday is the day the week begins — with worship, rest, and a reset. The W.A.I.T. strategy is for every young person who finds patience hard. Because it is hard. But waiting on God is not passive inaction. It is active trust.

W
Worship while you wait
Listen to uplifting music or praise
+

While you are waiting — for results, for a relationship to heal, for an answer to prayer, for your season to change — don't go silent towards God. Worship is not the reward for answered prayer. It is what you do in the waiting. It shifts your focus from what you don't have yet to who God already is. Put on something that lifts your eyes. Let it play. Let it do its work.

A
Accept God's timing
Trust that everything happens for a purpose
+

This is the hardest one. Accepting God's timing means releasing the idea that your timeline is the right one. It doesn't mean being passive about your goals. It means trusting that the God who sees the end from the beginning has a better vantage point than you do. His delays are not denials. His timing is not your timing — and that is a good thing.

I
Invite His presence
Pray and seek God daily
+

Waiting without connection becomes despair. But waiting with God — actively inviting His presence through prayer, through Scripture, through quiet — becomes formation. The waiting season is not wasted time. It is often where the deepest growth happens. But only if you invite Him into it. Don't wait in isolation. Wait in conversation with the One who holds the answer.

T
Trust in His plan
Believe that good things are coming
+

Trust is not a feeling. It is a choice. Choose to believe that God's plan for you is good — even when the circumstances say otherwise, even when you cannot see the next step, even when what you hoped for has not arrived. Jeremiah 29:11 was written to people in exile — people who had lost everything. "Plans to prosper you and not to harm you." He meant it then. He means it now.

Sunday reflection
What are you waiting for right now — and how are you waiting for it?
Are you waiting in worship, or in worry? In trust, or in resentment? The outcome may be the same. The person you become in the waiting is very different.
Mindset Monday
The S.H.I.E.L.D.
of Faith Formula
Faith shields you from life's challenges
"Take up the shield of faith, with which you can extinguish all the flaming arrows of the evil one." — Ephesians 6:16

Monday brings the week with it — its pressures, expectations, and the residue of everything unresolved from last week. The S.H.I.E.L.D. formula is your mental armour for the week ahead. Not a wish. A stance.

S
Stand firm in your beliefs
Value your integrity
+

Monday will bring moments where it is easier to go with the crowd, to stay silent when you should speak, to agree when you actually disagree. Standing firm is a daily choice. It starts with knowing what you believe — not just intellectually, but convictionally. If you don't know what you stand for, Monday will decide for you.

H
Hold onto God's promises
Remember His faithfulness
+

The week will bring circumstances that contradict what God has promised. Hold anyway. God's faithfulness is not circumstantial. It does not fluctuate with your Monday morning mood or your exam results. Take one promise into the week with you — one specific verse. Hold it. Let it hold you back.

I
Ignore negative influences
Choose positivity
+

This is not about being naive or sheltered. It is about being intentional. What goes in shapes what comes out. The content you consume, the voices you amplify, the conversations you enter — all of these are influencing your mindset, whether you are conscious of it or not. Choose Monday's inputs carefully.

E
Equip yourself with Scripture
Learn and meditate on God's Word
+

You would not go into an important event without preparation. Monday is an important event. Equip yourself. Read something before you open social media. Meditate on a verse before you respond to messages. Let God's Word shape the frame through which you interpret the day — before the day gets a chance to interpret itself.

L
Lift your faith through prayer
Talk to God about your worries
+

Prayer is not a religious chore. It is a conversation with the most capable Person in the universe about the things that matter most to you. Talk to God about your worries before you talk to anyone else about them. Not because other voices don't matter — but because His voice orients all the others.

D
Depend on God's strength
Rely on Him, never on yourself alone
+

Self-reliance feels strong until the moment it runs out. And it always runs out. Depending on God is not weakness — it is wisdom. It is the acknowledgement that you were never designed to carry the week alone. Philippians 4:13: "I can do all things through him who gives me strength." Not through my own cleverness. Through Him.

Monday reflection
Which letter of S.H.I.E.L.D. do you need most this week — and why?
Don't try to do all six on Monday. Pick one. Do it well. The shield is built one letter at a time.
Takeaway Tuesday
The B.R.I.D.G.E.
Strategy for Conflict
Build bridges, not walls
"If your brother or sister sins, go and point out their fault, just between the two of you." — Matthew 18:15

Conflict is inevitable — at home, at school, in friendships, in teams. What you do with conflict determines whether it destroys or deepens relationships. The B.R.I.D.G.E. strategy gives you a framework to work through it with grace.

B
Begin with prayer
Seek God's guidance before the conversation
+

Before you send the message, have the conversation, or confront the person — pray first. Not to get God on your side. To get yourself aligned with God's. Ask for humility, clarity, and the right words. The conversation that begins with prayer is a fundamentally different conversation than one driven by hurt or anger.

R
Review the situation calmly
Listen before reacting
+

Your first interpretation of a conflict is rarely the complete picture. Before you react, review. What actually happened — not just how it felt? What might the other person be carrying that you don't know about? Proverbs 18:17 says: "In a lawsuit the first to speak seems right, until someone comes forward and cross-examines." There is always another side.

I
Invite honest dialogue
Share your feelings respectfully
+

Use "I" statements rather than "you" accusations. "I felt hurt when..." lands very differently from "You always..." Invite the other person into a real conversation — not a verdict. You are not looking to win. You are looking to understand and to be understood. Those are different goals, and they produce different conversations.

D
Discuss solutions together
Find common ground
+

Once both sides have been heard, move toward solutions together. Not "here is what I need you to change" — but "what could we do differently going forward?" The shift from blame to problem-solving changes the entire dynamic. You are now on the same side of the table, looking at the problem together rather than at each other.

G
Give grace
Forgive and move forward
+

Grace is giving something that was not earned. Forgiveness is grace in action. It does not mean pretending the hurt wasn't real. It means choosing not to let the hurt define the relationship going forward. Forgiveness is primarily an act you do for yourself — it releases you from carrying the weight of the offence. Colossians 3:13: "Forgive as the Lord forgave you."

E
End with reconciliation
Restore the relationship wisely
+

Not every conflict ends in full reconciliation immediately — some take time. But the goal is always restoration, not victory. End the conversation with a commitment to the relationship, even if the process is not finished. A bridge is built one plank at a time. You don't need the whole bridge on Tuesday. You just need to lay the first plank.

Tuesday reflection
Is there a conflict you have been avoiding that this framework could help you enter?
The longer a conflict sits unaddressed, the heavier it becomes. The B.R.I.D.G.E. doesn't build itself. Someone has to take the first step.
Wisdom Wednesday
The T.A.B.L.E.
Method for Hospitality
Create a welcoming atmosphere
"When you give a banquet, invite the poor, the crippled, the lame, the blind." — Luke 14:13

Wednesday is the hinge of the week — the day to look outward. Hospitality is not about having a big house or making elaborate meals. It is the posture of making room for other people. The T.A.B.L.E. method is about how you treat people — especially those the world tends to overlook.

T
Take time to listen
Show genuine interest in the person in front of you
+

The most hospitable thing you can give another person is your full attention. Not your advice. Not your experience. Your attention. Ask a question. Then listen — not to respond, but to understand. Most people in your life are not looking for someone to fix their problems. They are looking for someone who will actually hear them.

A
Appreciate your friends
Make them feel valued
+

When did you last tell someone in your life that you appreciate them — specifically, and without a special occasion? Appreciation costs nothing and means everything. A text, a spoken sentence, a note. "I'm grateful for you because..." Name the specific thing. Generic appreciation lands softly. Specific appreciation lands deeply.

B
Break bread together
Share meals and stories
+

There is something about sharing food that lowers defences and opens conversations. Jesus consistently ate with people — with the excluded, the despised, the overlooked. The table is a posture, not just a piece of furniture. Who have you not eaten with recently that you should? A family member. A classmate who always eats alone. A neighbour you've never spoken to properly.

L
Lead with kindness
Be a positive influence — we all have influence
+

You have influence. Every person does. The question is not whether you are influencing the people around you — you are, constantly. The question is what direction you are pointing them. Kindness is not weakness. It is one of the most powerful forces available to a young person. People remember how you made them feel far longer than they remember what you said.

E
Encourage connection
Foster new friendships
+

Introduce people. Create spaces where connection can happen. Invite the person on the edge of the group into the centre. Hospitality is not just about welcoming people into your home — it is about welcoming them into your attention, your conversation, your care. Be the person who makes the room bigger, not smaller.

Wednesday reflection
Who in your world is on the edge of the group — and what would it look like to make room for them at your table this week?
The person who is easiest to overlook is often the one who most needs to be seen. That is rarely an accident. It is an invitation.
Thankful Thursday
The S.I.M.P.L.E.
Formula for Well-being
Pure air, sunlight, rest, and trust — a life in balance
"Do you not know that your bodies are temples of the Holy Spirit?" — 1 Corinthians 6:19

You are not just a mind or a soul. You are a whole person — body, mind, and spirit — and all three need tending. The S.I.M.P.L.E. formula is not complicated. That's the point. The basics, practised consistently, build the foundation for everything else.

S
Sunlight exposure
Spend time outdoors daily — breathe fresh air
+

Your body needs sunlight — it regulates your sleep, your mood, and your vitamin D levels. Fresh air resets the nervous system. Step outside today. Not for hours — even 10–15 minutes of intentional outdoor time can shift your energy and your mood. God made a beautiful world. You are allowed to be in it.

I
Improve diet naturally
Eat balanced, natural foods — avoid junk and fizzy drinks
+

What you eat affects how you think, how you feel, and how you perform. You cannot pour dirty fuel into a precision engine and expect peak performance. You don't need to be perfect about food — but one better choice today is better than one perfect day. Water over fizzy drinks. A fruit over a packet of crisps. Small shifts compound.

M
Maintain physical activity
Stay active — find a sport or activity you enjoy
+

Exercise is not punishment. It is one of the most effective tools you have for managing stress, improving sleep, sharpening thinking, and lifting mood. Find something you enjoy — walking, dancing, football, swimming, anything — and do it consistently. Three times a week. Start there.

P
Practice moderation
Balance screen time and rest
+

Your screen is not the enemy. How you use it is what matters. Moderation means conscious use — not mindless consumption. Set a time to put the phone down in the evening. Notice how it feels. The apps are designed to pull you back. Moderation is a form of courage in a world engineered for excess.

L
Leave time for rest
Prioritise sleep and downtime
+

Rest is not laziness. It is a biological need and a spiritual discipline. God rested on the seventh day — not because He was tired, but to model something important. Productive rest renews you. Sleep is when your brain consolidates memory, your body repairs itself, and your spirit settles. Protect your sleep like it matters. It does.

E
Embrace trust in God's care
Rely on Him for peace and strength
+

All the above helps. But the deepest well-being comes from trust — from knowing that you are held by Someone who does not sleep, does not tire, and does not lose track of you. Cast your anxiety on Him. Not once, but as often as it returns. 1 Peter 5:7: "Cast all your anxiety on him because he cares for you." He actually cares for you. Let that do its work.

Thursday reflection — give thanks
Which of the S.I.M.P.L.E. letters is most neglected in your life right now? What is one thing you could do about it today?
Well-being is not a goal you arrive at. It is a set of small daily choices. Pick one. Start today.
Focused Friday
The R.E.S.T.
Formula for Refreshment
Rest renews your soul
"He makes me lie down in green pastures, he leads me beside quiet waters, he refreshes my soul." — Psalm 23:2-3

Friday is the day to intentionally slow down before the weekend. Not to escape your responsibilities — to restore yourself so you can return to them with more to give. The R.E.S.T. formula is active, not passive. Real rest requires intention.

R
Release worries
Write down or talk about your concerns
+

Worries held inside keep you awake and on edge. Externalise them. Write them in a journal. Talk to a trusted friend. Pray them out loud. The act of expressing a worry often reduces its power over you. You are not ignoring the concern — you are releasing your grip on it so it stops gripping you. Philippians 4:6: present your requests to God, with thanksgiving.

E
Enjoy God's presence
Spend quiet time with Him
+

Not productive time. Not study time. Not prayer-list time. Simply being with God. Reading a psalm slowly. Sitting quietly. Letting your mind settle. The most profound rest is not the absence of activity — it is the presence of God. He leads beside quiet waters. Let Him lead you there today.

S
Still your mind
Meditate on God's promises and breathe deeply
+

A racing mind cannot receive. Stillness is a skill — and it takes practice. Start small. Two minutes of intentional breathing and one verse to meditate on. Psalm 46:10: "Be still, and know that I am God." The instruction is to stop striving long enough to know. Knowing God is what follows stillness, not what precedes it.

T
Trust God's plan
Believe that He is guiding you
+

Real rest is only possible when you trust the One who holds what you cannot control. If you are carrying the weight of everything — every outcome, every relationship, every uncertainty — you cannot actually rest. Trust is what transfers the weight. "Cast all your anxiety on him." That transfer is an act of will. Do it intentionally. Then rest.

Friday reflection
What would it look like to genuinely rest this evening — not just stop working, but actually restore?
Rest is not automatic. It is chosen. What do you need to release to make room for it?
Serene Saturday
The S.T.R.I.V.E.
Strategy for Excellence
Strive for your best in all you do
"Whatever you do, work at it with all your heart, as working for the Lord, not for human masters." — Colossians 3:23

Excellence is not perfectionism. It is giving your best — consistently, deliberately, and for the right reasons. The S.T.R.I.V.E. strategy is for the young person who wants to excel — not just to impress others, but to honour God with the gifts and opportunities they have been given.

S
Set realistic goals
Aim high but stay grounded
+

A goal without a plan is a wish. A goal that is impossibly out of reach becomes discouragement. Set goals that stretch you without breaking you. High enough to require growth. Specific enough to track. Time-bound enough to create momentum. And then — crucially — write them down. The goal that lives only in your head rarely survives contact with Monday.

T
Train diligently
Practice consistently and improve daily
+

Excellence is built in the unglamorous repetition of daily practice. There is no substitute for consistent effort over time. The musician who plays scales every day. The athlete who trains when no one is watching. The student who reads before anyone is impressed. Diligence is the bridge between potential and performance.

R
Review your progress
Celebrate wins and learn from failures
+

Unreflected effort is just busy-ness. Build in regular review. What worked? What didn't? What would you do differently? Celebration is not vanity — it is acknowledgement that progress happened, and it motivates the next step. Learning from failure is not wallowing — it is extracting the lesson so the effort was not wasted.

I
Innovate and adapt
Be creative and flexible
+

The world changes. What worked last year may not work this year. Excellence requires adaptability. Be willing to try a different approach, to learn something new, to change the method while holding the goal. Rigidity in a changing world is not strength — it is fragility. Creativity and flexibility are themselves skills worth developing.

V
Value quality over speed
Do your best — not just to finish first
+

The world rewards speed. God rewards faithfulness. Quality outlasts speed every time. Work done well — with care, attention, and integrity — produces something that lasts. Work done fast to impress produces something that fades. Focus on doing it right. The time it takes is part of what makes the result trustworthy.

E
Excel for God's glory
Let every effort honour Him
+

This is the one that changes everything else. When you do your work for God — not for grades, not for approval, not to beat someone else — the quality of your motivation changes. Colossians 3:23: "Whatever you do, work at it with all your heart, as working for the Lord." Whatever you is doing today — let it be an act of worship. Then do it excellently.

Saturday reflection
In which area of your life are you settling for good enough when you could be striving for excellent — and what is one step that would change that this week?
Excellence is not about comparing yourself to others. It is about comparing who you are to who you are capable of becoming — and closing that gap, one day at a time.
