Day 1 Workbook · Identity
Deliverable: your written identity statement
01
The itch
Labels inventory
What are the labels others have given you — at home, at school, online, in relationships — that you've quietly started to believe about yourself?
Write them honestly. You're not agreeing with them — you're naming what's been living rent-free in your head. Naming a label is the first step to deciding whether it stays.
02
Your most alive moment
Describe a specific moment when you felt most like yourself — most free, most present, most alive. Where were you? What were you doing? Who were you with?
Don't overthink it. It can be small. A conversation, a project, a place. The moment matters more than the size of it. This is a clue about who you actually are.
03
Your effortless strengths
List 3 things you do that feel natural — things that come easily to you even when they're hard work for others. What do people come to you for? What do you lose track of time doing?
Strengths are not the same as skills. A skill is learned. A strength is wired in. You've had these since before anyone told you they were valuable.
04
God's view of you
Read Psalm 139:13–16 slowly — once for information, once to let it speak. Then write: if God described you to someone based on how He made you — not your mistakes, not your highlights — what do you think He'd say?
This isn't a theology exam. It's a permission slip. You are allowed to see yourself through kinder, truer eyes than the world has given you.
05
Day 1 Deliverable — Identity Statement
Complete this in your own words: "I am someone who ____________. I was made to ____________. The truth about me — beyond what others have said — is ____________."
This is your first draft. It doesn't need to be perfect. It needs to be honest. You will build on this on Day 3.
→ After writing this, you can say who you are without borrowing anyone else's words.

Scripture anchor — read this before Prompt 4
"You made all the delicate, inner parts of my body and knit me together in my mother's womb... Every day of my life was recorded in your book. Every moment was laid out before a single day had passed."

Overnight challenge
Write "I AM ___________" somewhere you will see it first thing tomorrow.
Your mirror. Your phone lock screen. A sticky note on your door. Fill in the blank with something true about you that has nothing to do with what you've achieved or what others have said. This is practice — saying it to yourself before you say it to anyone else.

The beliefs quietly running your life
Core teaching: everyone operates on values — but most people's values are inherited, not chosen. They came from parents, culture, wounds, and habit. And inherited values don't protect you — they just run in the background, making decisions before you consciously do.

Deliverable: your top 5 values named + written values statement
01
The itch
Values inventory
Circle every value that resonates — don't filter, go fast. Then eliminate to 10. Then narrow to your top 5. Write them below and next to each one, write one word for why it matters to you.
Integrity · Faith · Family · Courage · Creativity · Service · Excellence · Freedom · Honesty · Compassion · Justice · Loyalty · Growth · Humility · Adventure · Community · Discipline · Kindness · Leadership · Peace · Purpose · Resilience · Wisdom · Authenticity · Joy · Responsibility · Generosity · Boldness · Gratitude · Love
02
Proof — your #1 value in action
For your #1 value: write about a specific, real moment when you lived it out — even when it cost you something. What happened? What did you choose? What did it feel like afterwards?
If you can't think of a moment, ask yourself honestly: have I actually lived this value, or do I just admire it from a distance? Both answers are useful.
03
The itch
The tension — when you violated a value
Think of a recent decision that felt wrong afterwards — a yes you regretted, a path that didn't fit. Looking back honestly: which of your values did that decision violate? What were you actually choosing against?
This isn't about guilt. It's about data. The discomfort you felt was your values working — trying to protect something you care about. Now you know what that is.
04
God's view — where is your heart?
Read Matthew 6:19–21 slowly. Then answer honestly: where is your heart most invested right now — your time, attention, and energy? Is that where you want it to be? What would it look like to align your heart with your values?
No performance here. Just honesty. The point isn't to feel bad about the gap — it's to see it clearly so you can choose differently.
05
Day 2 Deliverable — Values Statement
Write your personal values statement — 2 to 3 sentences. Start with: "I am someone who believes in ______. I will live this out by ______. When life gets hard and I start to drift, I will return to ______."
This is your decision filter. Any time you face a choice that doesn't feel right — come back to this. It will tell you what you're protecting and what to choose.

Day 3 Workbook · Purpose
Deliverable: your written North Star Statement
01
The intersection — strengths meet values
Look back at your Day 1 workbook (your strengths and your most alive moment) and your Day 2 workbook (your top 5 values). Where do they overlap? What kind of person do those two things point to? What pattern keeps showing up?
You're not writing a career plan. You're looking for a thread — the thing that runs through who you are across different contexts. Name it even if it feels incomplete.
02
The itch
What breaks your heart
What situation, problem, or injustice in the world makes you feel something deeply — anger, grief, urgency? Why does it bother you more than it bothers others? Be specific — not "poverty" but what aspect of it, where, and why it pulls at you.
Pain is often a pointer. The thing that breaks your heart is often the thing you were designed to do something about. It doesn't have to be dramatic — it just has to be real.
03
Dream freely — no filters
If fear, money, other people's opinions, and the pressure to be "realistic" were completely removed — what would you be doing with your life in 10 years? Who would you be helping? What would you be building? What would a Thursday afternoon look like?
Don't edit yourself. Write the version you actually want — not the responsible one, not the safe one, not the one that wouldn't embarrass you if someone read it. The real one.
04
One next step — this week
What is one specific thing — just one — you could do this week that would move you one step closer to the person you described in Prompt 3? Make it small enough to actually do by Sunday.
Not "read more" — what book, starting when? Not "exercise" — what, where, what day? The more specific it is, the more likely it happens. A conversation. A decision. A habit. A message you've been putting off.
05
Day 3 Deliverable — North Star Statement
Write your personal North Star Statement — 2 to 3 sentences that capture who you are, what you stand for, and where you're moving. Use present tense. Write it as if it's already true — because in God's eyes, it is.
Start with: "I am ______. I exist to ______. I will know I'm on track when ______." This is not a final answer. It's a stake in the ground. It will grow as you do.
